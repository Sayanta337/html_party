So, What is a Solar Eclipse?

A solar eclipse occurs when the Moon passes between Earth and the Sun, blocking all or part of the Sun's light from reaching Earth. Just like shown in the picture below.

A solar eclipse happens during a new moon phase, when the Moon aligns itself directly between the Earth and the Sun. This alignment casts a shadow on Earth, which can be observed from specific locations.
There are two parts to the Moon's shadow :

  • Penumbra : The area where only a portion of the Sun is obscured, leading to a partial solar eclipse.
  • Umbra : The area where the Sun is completely obscured by the Moon, resulting in a total solar eclipse.
